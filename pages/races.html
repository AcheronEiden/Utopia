The Utopia World Manual

A Tapestry of Sentient Beings

Utopia, and the planes beyond, pulsate with the life of a multitude of sentient races, each contributing to the vibrant tapestry of the multiverse.

Humans

The most widespread race, renowned for their adaptability and boundless thirst for knowledge. Humans are known as the symbol of justice and corruption.

Elves

Possess a deep connection to nature and often reside in hidden enclaves. Elves tend to be introspective and prefer solitude, fiercely guarding their territories when threatened.

Dwarves

Stoic and industrious, dwarves hold a profound respect for tradition and craftsmanship. Their love for forging and mining makes them unparalleled blacksmiths, crafting magical artifacts of exceptional quality. Dwarves are known for their hearty appetites and legendary love of a good beer.

2.1 Beyond the Common Races

While humans, elves, and dwarves may be the most widespread, other fascinating races inhabit the planes:

Vampires

Rare and shrouded in mystery, vampires are often viewed as the embodiment of evil. In their early stages, they rely on feeding on other races to grow stronger and ascend in rank. For higher-ranked vampires, blood becomes less of a necessity but remains a ritualistic symbol of nobility. These immortal beings cannot procreate naturally and instead turn others into vampires through a blood-sharing ritual.

Demons and Devils

These ancient races reside in their respective infernal realms - the Abyss and Hell. Demons embody raw chaos and destruction, reveling in carnage and violence. Devils, in contrast, are cunning strategists who seek to dominate and enslave. The animosity between these two factions fuels the relentless war known as the "Battlefield of Despair."

Dragons

A primordial race of immense power and unmatched arrogance, dragons are the undisputed apex predators. Their bodies are naturally attuned to magic, amplifying their formidable physical prowess. Dragons are incredibly rare, and their pride often leads them to view other races with disdain. These magnificent creatures are said to possess the power of an Eighth Order Being at their peak with the potential to become Ninth Order Beings. Due to Dragons innate abilities and sensitivity to mana, they are exceptional trackers. There is a saying that goes like this:

You would prefer a God chase you rather than a Dragon. Those monsters don't stop.

2.2 True Names and the Source of Their Power

In the realm of Utopia, beings of exceptional power can be bestowed with True Names by higher beings, often deities or cosmic entities. These True Names are not mere labels; they are imbued with immense power, elevating the bearer's abilities far beyond their natural limits. The potency of a True Name varies depending on its meaning and the nature of the being who bestowed it. A True Name associated with death, for example, would grant its bearer exceptional control over death-related powers, potentially even elevating them to become an embodiment of death itself.

2.3 The Cultivation Path: From Novice to Godhood

The cultivation path is divided into distinct ranks, each representing a significant increase in power and understanding. The lowest rank is the First-Order Being, followed by Second, Third, Fourth, Fifth, and Sixth Order Beings. At the Sixth Order, a mage awakens the ability to generate mana within their bodies, reducing their reliance on external mana.

Beyond the Sixth Order lies the realms of the truly powerful. Seventh and Eighth Order Beings, powerhouses that command immense strength and influence. The Eighth Order, also known as the Demigod level, marks a pivotal point, where mages transcend mortal limitations. The pinnacle of power is the Ninth Order, granting the title of Minor God. Those who reach this level gain minor influence over certain Laws of the universe, allowing them to influence those Laws themselves.

Rumors say that beyond the Ninth Order lies even greater heights, such as the True God, Primordial God, and Principality ranks. Especially important is the difference between the Principality and other ranks. When a Primordial God sheds their skin, they become the personification of an integral part of the cosmic (for example the Principality of Death that is the personification of Death). As a Principality is integral to the existence or non-existence of the universe, a Primordial God that reaches the Principality rank cannot be erased from existence and only absorbed by other personifications.

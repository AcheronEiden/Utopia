Aethelgard: A History Forged in Blood and Steel

The Major Events of Aethelgard: A Brief Timeline

The "peace" of the current era is but a brief, quiet moment in a long and violent history. The continent of Aethelgard has been shaped by catastrophic wars, legendary betrayals, and the slow, grinding politics of survival. To understand the present, one must first understand the past.

The First Hollow War

Centuries ago, a massive abyssal rift tore open in the heart of the continent, unleashing a tide of demonic horrors. This event, known as the First Hollow War, led to the fall of the ancient Kingdom of Edra and created the blighted, lawless territory now known as the Gray Wastes. It was during this desperate war that the first "Alliance of Convenience" was forged. The fledgling human tribes, facing annihilation, formally allied with the Dwarven Kingdom of Khaz'Grorum. The humans provided the manpower, the dwarves provided the enchanted steel, and together they managed to seal the rift, but at an unimaginable cost.

The Sunstone Tragedy

Following the war, the alliance soured. Driven by greed, a human warlord betrayed the Elves of the Whispering Woods, slaughtering a delegation of their diplomats to steal the Sunstone, a sacred Elven artifact. This single act of treachery shattered the trust between the races. The Elves, in their grief and fury, retreated into absolute isolation, their hearts hardened against all outsiders. This event is the root of the deep-seated mistrust that plagues the continent to this day, a wound that has never truly healed.

The Rise of the Empires

With the Elves in seclusion, the human tribes, now unified, rapidly expanded, forming the Southern Empire. In the west, the ancient vampire clans, seeing an opportunity, consolidated their power and established the aggressive, expansionist Blood Empire. This marked the beginning of the current era: a tense, three-way cold war between the two great empires and the isolationist Elves, with the Dwarven Kingdom acting as the indispensable, yet vulnerable, fourth power.

Cultures of the Realm

The Southern Empire: Gilded Cages

The streets are a vibrant, multicultural melting pot. However, beneath this gilded surface lies a ruthless, meritocratic society. Power is everything. The Great Houses engage in shadowy political games, while commoners must be cunning and resourceful to climb the social ladder. It is a place of great opportunity and equally great danger, where a kind word can hide a sharp dagger.

The Dwarven Kingdom: The Rhythm of the Forge

Dwarven society is built on three pillars: the clan, the forge, and the ancestors. Life in the subterranean halls of Khaz'Grorum moves to the steady, rhythmic beat of the smith's hammer. Their culture is one of tradition, honor, and an unwavering respect for craftsmanship. A Dwarf's worth is measured not by their wealth, but by the quality of their work and their loyalty to their clan. While they are famously stubborn and slow to trust outsiders, they are fiercely loyal to their allies and will remember a debt for generations.

The Elven Enclaves: The Great Silence

To be an Elf is to live in a state of deep, melancholic connection to the natural world. Since the Sunstone Tragedy, their society has become deeply insular and quiet. They create breathtaking works of art, music, and magic, but these are shared only amongst themselves. They view the frantic pace of human ambition with a weary sadness, seeing it as a fleeting flame destined to burn itself out. Their relationship with the forest is symbiotic; they are its guardians, and it is the source of their immense power and their eternal sorrow.
